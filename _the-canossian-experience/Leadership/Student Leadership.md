<h2><strong>Student Leadership at SACSS</strong></h2>
<p>Rooted in the Canossian spirit of humility and service, we nurture student
leaders who lead with the heart and serve with purpose.</p>
<p>At SACSS, leadership is not a position, but a calling. We form our students
to be servant leaders — women of strength, compassion, and integrity, who
are committed to serving others and making a positive difference in the
world.</p>
<p>Guided by our school vision, our student leaders embody:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Compassion</strong> (caring deeply for others, especially those
in need)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Commitment</strong> (persevering with courage and responsibility)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Competence</strong> (striving for excellence in all that they do)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Cheerfulness</strong> (remaining hopeful and positive, even in
adversity)</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>We believe that true leadership is not about authority, but about placing
others before self and making a meaningful impact through service.</p>
<p></p>
<h3><strong>Leading Through Strengths, Serving with Purpose</strong></h3>
<p>At SACSS, we believe that every student possesses unique strengths, abilities,
and talents. Each girl brings her own leadership style and potential, and
we are committed to helping her discover and harness these strengths.</p>
<p>Through the use of VIA Character Strengths, students gain deeper self-awareness
of their personal qualities. They learn to:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Identify their top strengths through a customised report</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Apply these strengths to lead and serve others</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Build confidence in their own leadership identity</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>By recognising and leveraging their strengths, our students are empowered
to make a positive impact within their spheres of influence and contribute
meaningfully to the community.</p>
<p></p>
<h3><strong>A Progressive Leadership Journey</strong></h3>
<p>Our leadership development programme adopts a progressive approach, with
each level focusing on a different aspect of leadership growth.</p>
<h4><strong>Secondary 1: Personal Leadership</strong></h4>
<p>Students begin by learning to lead themselves. Through Form Teacher lessons
on executive functioning, they develop discipline, responsibility, and
self-management skills that lay the foundation for effective leadership.</p>
<h4><strong>Secondary 2: Peer Leadership&nbsp;</strong></h4>
<p>The Leaders-in-Training (L-i-T) and Peer Support Leaders-in-Training (PSL-i-T)
programmes are key platforms for selected Secondary 2 students to begin
their leadership journey.</p>
<p>In this programme, students:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Are mentored by senior student leaders</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Gain hands-on experience in organising school-wide projects</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Participate in reflection and feedback sessions to support growth</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>The programme emphasises:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Self-awareness</strong> — understanding strengths and areas for
growth</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Positivity</strong> — maintaining an optimistic outlook</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Growth mindset</strong> — embracing challenges as opportunities</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Role-modelling</strong> — setting a positive example for peers</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Students also experience a strong sense of community, forming meaningful
connections with peers, seniors and teachers. Through opportunities for
co-agency, they are empowered to take initiative, co-create solutions,
and build confidence as emerging leaders.</p>
<p>
<br>
<br>
</p>
<h4><strong>Secondary 3: Team Leadership</strong></h4>
<p>As students grow in their leadership journey, they may take on formal
leadership roles based on their readiness and commitment to the school’s
values.</p>
<p>These roles include:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Student Councillors</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Peer Support Leaders</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>CCA Leaders</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Catholic Activities Council Leaders</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>In these positions, students learn to lead teams, manage responsibilities,
and contribute meaningfully to the school community.</p>
<h4><strong>Secondary 4: Thought Leadership</strong></h4>
<p>As senior leaders, Secondary 4 students take on the role of mentors to
their juniors. They guide Secondary 3 leaders, share their experiences,
and model values-driven leadership.</p>
<p>At this stage, students develop thought leadership — influencing others
through their ideas, perspectives, and example, while leaving a lasting
legacy in the school.</p>
