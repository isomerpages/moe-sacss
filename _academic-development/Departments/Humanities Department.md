<h3><strong>Objectives</strong></h3>
<p>
<br>The Humanities Department aims to develop informed, concerned and participative
students through an inquiry-based approach, where students critically explore
real-world issues, interpret and evaluate evidence, appreciate diverse
perspectives, and demonstrate reasoned judgement and responsible action
towards a sustainable and inclusive future.</p>

<h3><strong>Approach</strong></h3>
<p>Our Humanities teaching adopts an inquiry-based approach, where students
learn through structured investigation tasks that require them to explore
authentic questions, analyse evidence and construct reasoned responses.</p>
<p>Students engage in collaborative learning, discussing and refining ideas
through peer interaction. They are also supported in developing metacognitive
awareness through peer and self-assessment, enabling them to monitor their
understanding and improve their work.</p>
<p>Through these practices, students develop critical thinking, becoming
more confident in evaluating perspectives, making judgements and applying
their learning to real-world contexts.</p>

<p><strong>Programmes</strong>
</p>
<p>Our Humanities programmes are designed to extend learning beyond the classroom
through authentic, inquiry-based experiences that develop critical and
reflective learners, who engage actively with issues that matter to them
and their community.</p>
<p><strong>Experiential Learning: Geography Fieldwork</strong>
</p>
<p>Lower Secondary students participate in Geography fieldwork, where they
investigate real-world environments and sustainability issues through an
inquiry approach. By applying geographical skills such as observation,
data collection and analysis, students deepen their understanding of human-environment
relationships and develop environmental responsibility.</p>
<p><strong>Stretching Passion and Excellence in Humanities</strong>
</p>
<p>Students with interest and aptitude in the Humanities are provided with
opportunities to deepen their learning and extend their thinking through
a range of enrichment platforms:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Heritage Trails (Cluster Collaboration): Students explore Singapore’s
historical and cultural landscapes, making connections between the past
and present while engaging in inquiry based learning.&nbsp; The process
itself enables students to appreciate inter-disciplinary learning within
the Humanities subjects.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Model United Nations (MUN): Students engage in global issues, represent
different perspectives, and develop skills in research, negotiation and
public speaking.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>NUS Geography Challenge: Students apply geographical concepts and analytical
skills to real-world problems, competing alongside peers at a national
level.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Student Agency in Action: Racial Harmony Day Programme (G1 Social Studies)&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p>Students design and implement a varied range of Racial Harmony Day activities
each year, drawing on their inquiry into identity, diversity and harmony
to reflect student voice and perspectives.</p>
