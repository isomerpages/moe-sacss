<h3><strong>Objectives</strong></h3>
<p>The SACSS Physical Education (PE) Department aims to develop our students'
physical, mental, emotional, and psychosocial well-being through a systematic
and inclusive, modular PE program. Our goal is to develop physically competent
and confident individuals who enjoy a lifetime of active and healthy living,
safely and responsibly, while supporting the development of 21st Century
Competencies (21CC).</p>
<p></p>
</td>
</tr>

<h3><strong>Approach</strong></h3>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Modular approach to lessons covering Physical Activity (Sports &amp; Games),
Physical Health and Safety (PHS), Outdoor Education (OE)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Students learn a variety of games categorised into invasion, net/wall,
striking/fielding, target, and skills-based games</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Outdoor Education engages students’ spirit of adventure and develops their
resilience</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Physical Health and Safety support students in their development of healthy
lifestyle practices and pursuit of a lifetime of active and healthy living</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Initiatives and activities beyond curriculum to promote healthier living</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Fun and engaging, promoting movement</p>
</li>
</ul>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<h3><strong>Programmes</strong></h3>
<p>With the knowledge and skills gained through the modules, students are
required to apply their learning in authentic contexts through a culminating
activity for each module.</p>
<p>The department also organizes two school-wide events: Jogathon and Sports
Day. Jogathon, our annual cross-country event, provides an opportunity
for students to come together as a class and compete in long-distance running,
developing resilience. Our annual Sports Day also features students competing
in shorter distance races and team events, ultimately vying to be champion
classes, promoting school spirit.</p>
<p>To promote and support healthier living, the department also organises
the MOVE programme and Free Play initiatives, providing opportunities and
encouraging students to be more physically active.</p>
