<h3><strong>Objectives</strong><br></h3>
<p>Our school’s Science curriculum adheres to the Ministry of Education’s
Science Curriculum Framework and utilizes the Scientific Inquiry Approach
to meet both national and international standards. The primary aim is to
cultivate critical, analytical, and inventive thinkers who possess curiosity
and a positive attitude towards science. By studying Secondary Science,
students will acquire vital knowledge and skills across various disciplines,
including Biology, Chemistry, and Physics, enabling them to understand
and apply scientific concepts in real-life situations.</p>
<p></p>

<h3><strong>Approach</strong></h3>
<p><strong>Hands-On Investigations</strong>: Students engage in hands-on
investigations and experiments in realistic settings.</p>
<p><strong>Collaborative Learning</strong>: Learning occurs both inside and
outside the classroom, promoting collaboration with peers and the use of
technology.</p>
<p><strong>Laboratory Enhancement</strong>: Laboratory sessions complement
classroom teaching, providing practical experiences.</p>
<p><strong>Spirit of Inquiry</strong>: Specially planned programs encourage
a spirit of inquiry and ethical behavior, nurturing the next generation
of scientists.</p>

<h3><strong>Programmes</strong></h3>
<p>
<br>
</p>
<p><strong>Learning Beyond the Classroom<br></strong> We believe that learning
science extends beyond the classroom walls. Outdoor experiences bring science
to life, and collaborations with organizations like South East CDC, NEA,
NParks, and the Science Centre enhance learning through workshops and competitions.
Field trips to locations such as the Yakult factory and NEWater Visitor
Centre offer hands-on adventures that complement classroom learning.</p>
<p><strong>Science Research Programme (SRP)<br></strong> The Science Research
Programme (SRP) equips students with essential skills such as critical
thinking, effective communication, data analysis, and problem-solving through
hands-on experiments, fieldwork, and independent research projects. This
programme nurtures curiosity and creativity, allowing students to work
closely with school-based mentors to conduct their own experiments and
enhance their understanding of scientific principles. Students are also
encouraged to present their findings through reports, presentations, and
posters, effectively communicating their ideas and preparing for future
academic and professional challenges. Passionate participants can look
forward to engaging in competitions like the Singapore Science and Engineering
Fair, providing excellent opportunities to showcase their talents and achievements.</p>
