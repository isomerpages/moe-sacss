<h2><strong>Forming the Whole Person</strong></h2>
<p>At St. Anthony’s Canossian Secondary School (SACSS), we nurture each girl
in her own way, forming hearts, enlightening minds, and empowering spirits.
Rooted in the vision of St. Magdalene of Canossa, we form the whole person
and develop Canossians of Influence who are competent, compassionate, committed,
and cheerful.</p>
<p>This commitment is realised through Character and Citizenship Education
(CCE) as a process of character formation, which is intentionally designed
to shape how students reflect, discern, and act. In a complex world, they
encounter situations that require them to manage relationships and make
decisions. A safe and supportive environment enables them to reflect on
their experiences, consider different perspectives, and better understand
themselves and others. As they progress through the levels, they deepen
their sense of identity, build meaningful relationships, and grow in their
ability to respond thoughtfully.</p>
<h2><strong>Learning through Experience</strong></h2>
<p>At the heart of our approach is a metacognitive process—Notice, Think,
Choose. Students notice their thoughts and emotions, consider perspectives,
and choose with intention, aligning their actions with their values. This
is developed through CCE lessons designed as formation experiences, where
each lesson begins with a meaningful trigger that connects learning to
real-life situations. Through guided dialogue, students make sense of their
experiences and take ownership of their decisions, with student voice shaping
the process as they share perspectives, listen to others, and contribute
meaningfully to class learning.</p>
<p>Learning extends beyond the classroom through Student Development Experiences
such as VIA, CCA, Student Leadership Development, National Education, and
level activities. These structured experiences provide authentic opportunities
to apply values, develop character, and grow as active citizens. In these
contexts, students work with others, navigate challenges, support their
peers, and act on what they believe is right, translating reflection into
lived experiences.</p>
<h2><strong>Growing through Reflection and Relationships</strong></h2>
<p>Across all contexts, students are supported in a caring environment where
they feel safe to share and grow. The class committee fosters a PERMAH
culture that supports well-being and belonging. Students draw on their
VIA Character Strengths to act with purpose and contribute to others. Supportive
peer relationships and the resilience framework of I Am, I Have, I Can
help them grow in confidence and strengthen their sense of identity.</p>
<p>Students reflect monthly through <em>My Canossian Story</em>, an online
journal that captures their journey, helping them recognise patterns in
their thinking and deepen self-understanding.</p>
<p>Through the intentional design of these experiences, students develop
habits of reflection and decision-making that shape their thinking, relationships,
and actions. Ultimately, they make choices grounded in their values and
contribute to the greater good, growing into active citizens who care for
others and make a positive difference in society.</p>
